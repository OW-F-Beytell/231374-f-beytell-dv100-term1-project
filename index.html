The White Stripes

The White Stripes were a groundbreaking rock duo from Detroit, consisting of Jack (guitar and vocals) and Meg (drums) White. Their raw, blues-infused garage rock sound and striking visual style captured the attention of music fans and critics worldwide, with songs like "Seven Nation Army", "Fell In Love With A Girl" and "The Hardest Button To Button" becoming rock classics. Despite disbanding in 2011, their impact on modern rock music continues to be felt today, cementing their place as one of the most influential bands of the 21st century.
